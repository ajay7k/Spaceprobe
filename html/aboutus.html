About Us

SpaceProbe Pvt Ltd is a pioneering company founded with the mission to bridge the gap between cutting-edge research and real-world applications. We are dedicated to developing innovative tools and services that empower communities, industries, and institutions.

Our expertise spans space weather alerts and forecasting, geospatial intelligence through GIS and remote sensing, and advanced technological solutions, all designed to promote sustainable growth and informed decision-making.

Beyond technology, we are equally committed to nurturing a scientific temperament in society. Through science outreach, exhibitions, corporate events, workshops, training schools, and citizen science programs, we strive to make science more accessible and engaging for all.

By uniting scientific expertise with technological innovation, SpaceProbe seeks to build a strong ecosystem where knowledge, exploration, and application move together, helping communities not just understand space but truly benefit from it.

Our Mission

At Spaceprobe, we aim to bridge fundamental space plasma research with applied technologies. From studying solar eruptions and interplanetary structures to forecasting space weather, we focus on improving our understanding of Sun–Earth interactions and their impact on technology-driven society.

Our Technology

We combine real-time data analytics, advanced simulations, and AI-driven prediction tools to create next-generation solutions in space weather monitoring. Our technology stack includes spacecraft data pipelines, modeling of solar wind–magnetosphere interactions, and predictive systems for geomagnetic storms and ionospheric disturbances.

Our Story

Founded by passionate researchers, Spaceprobe began as a vision to unify academic excellence with industry-driven innovation. What started as a research initiative in heliophysics has now grown into a collaborative platform engaging with universities, research labs, and space agencies worldwide.

Today & Beyond

Spaceprobe continues to expand its research and applications in:

  • Solar wind and heliospheric plasma studies
  • Magnetic flux rope and plasmoid dynamics
  • Real-time space weather forecasting
  • Ground-to-space impact studies (power grids, satellites, navigation)
  • Collaborations with global research centers and agencies
